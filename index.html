AHP Decision Analyzer

Analytic Hierarchy Process — rank alternatives across a weighted criteria hierarchy

1

Define the decision

Criteria hierarchy

Add criteria, then nest sub-criteria under any of them with + sub. Leaf nodes (no children) are what the alternatives get compared against.

Alternatives

The options you are choosing between (e.g. Laptop A, Laptop B).

    2

    Make your comparisons

    Pick a respondent (or add more for a group decision), then fill in their pairwise comparisons. First weigh the criteria, then score the alternatives under each leaf criterion (Saaty scale: 1 = equal, 9 = extreme). The Group (avg) view aggregates everyone via the geometric mean.

    What the gauge means — Saaty intensity: 1 Equal 3 Moderate 5 Strong 7 Very strong 9 Extreme The centre means the two are equally important. Drag the gauge — or tap ◀ / ▶ — toward the option you consider more important; the further you go, the stronger that preference. The value shown is the left-to-right ratio: e.g. 3 means the left option is 3× as important, 1/3 means the right one is (the group average shows a decimal, below 1 when the right option wins).
    3

    Results

    Vanilla HTML, CSS & JS — no dependencies. Priorities use the principal eigenvector (power iteration); leaf weights propagate through the hierarchy; consistency uses Saaty's CR.
